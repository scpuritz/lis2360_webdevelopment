Bowser

Bowser, or King Koopa, is the main antagonist of Nintendo's Mario franchise. He's known to have a habit of kidnapping the royalty of the Mushroom kingdom, and he often appears as the "final boss" in any Mario game. In Super Smash Bros. Melee, Bowser is regarded as a "low-tier" character, and is "a mighty foe that trades speed for pure power."

Princess Peach

Princess Peach, or Princess Toadstool, is the princess of the fictional Mushroom Kingdom in the Mario franchise. She is constantly under attack by Bowser, and is the love interest of Mario. In Super Smash Bros. Melee, Peach is regarded as one of the best characters in the game. Her unique aerial attacks and incredible precision makes her a top choice for any Smasher. According to the Melee instruction manual, Peach is a "princess with royal recovery abilities."

All charcters created equally. Or are they?

To newcomers, all characters in Super Smash Bros. Melee are playable and balanced. However, the thriving competitive Melee scene has deemed otherwise. Many top players create charcter "tier lists," essentially ranking characters in the game from best to worst based on their opinions and experiences with the game. Fox, due to his incredible speed and power, is regarded as the best in the game. Fox is followed by characters like Marth, the swordsman, and Jigglypuff, an emerging favorite for the new "best character in the game."

Fountain of Dreams

Loosely based off the Fountain of Dreams in the Kirby Universe, this stage in Super Smash Bros. Melee is one of the most beloved. It is beautifully designed and is well known for having a mirror-like effect as characters walk across the water, a relatively innovative visual for an early 2000s console. Fountain of Dreams is one of the most popular stages for competitive play, due to its general balance: the moving platforms can prevent camping, and the shape of the edges of the stage prevent characters from getting stuck underneath them. Additionally, the pillar on the bottom of the stage allows characters who can wall jump to improve their recoveries, while simultaneously preventing players from stalling by traveling underneath the stage.
